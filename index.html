Low-Sugar Protein Oat Balls
No-bake, honey-sweetened, zero sugar. Makes 12 balls (~32g each).
⏱ 15 min 🧊 No bake 🍫 12 servings

Ingredients
  • Rolled oats149g5¼ oz
  • Vanilla protein powder60g2⅛ oz
  • Natural peanut or almond butterno added sugar120g4¼ oz
  • Wholesome Yum Zero Sugar Honey50g1¾ oz
  • Ground flaxseed19g⅔ oz
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder9g⅓ oz
  • Vanilla extract5ml1 tsp
  • Cinnamon½ tsp½ tsp
  • Salt1 pinch1 pinch
Steps
  1. 1

    Combine rolled oats, protein powder, ground flaxseed, cocoa powder, cinnamon, and salt in a large bowl. Whisk until evenly mixed — this prevents protein powder clumps later.

  2. 2

    Add nut butter, zero sugar honey, and vanilla extract to the dry mix. Stir well until a thick dough forms. The honey substitute acts as the binder — no extra liquid needed.

  3. 3

    Press a small amount between your fingers — it should hold together without crumbling or sticking. Too dry? Add 5–10g more honey. Too wet? Add a small handful of oats.

  4. 4

    Scoop ~32g of dough per ball and roll between your palms. If the dough is sticking, chill for 10 minutes first — cold dough is much easier to handle.

  5. 5

    Place on a parchment-lined plate or container. Cover and refrigerate overnight (minimum 30 min). Overnight is best — oats fully absorb moisture and balls firm up properly.

🧊 Storage

Up to 1 week in an airtight container in the fridge. To freeze: lay individually on a tray first, then transfer to a bag — lasts 2 months frozen.

📊 Macro note

Macros vary by brand. Whey + peanut butter = lower calorie end. Plant protein + almond butter = slightly higher.

Passato di Verdure with Shredded Chicken
High-protein blended vegetable soup. 3 portions, built for the freezer.
⏱ ~50 min ❄️ Freezable 🍽 3 servings

Ingredients

  • Tuscan kale (cavolo nero)200g7 oz

  • Leekwhite + light green, sliced150g5¼ oz
  • Water or light stockto coverto cover
  • Protein
  • Boneless skinless chicken breastraw475g~1 lb
  • Fats & Finish
  • Olive oil7ml½ tbsp

  • Seasoning
  • Salt + black pepperto tasteto taste
  • Bay leaf + garlic cloveoptionaloptionaloptional
Steps
  1. 1

    Poach the chicken first. Place breasts in a single layer, cover with cold water or stock, add salt and optional aromatics. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook 12–15 min. Turn heat off and rest 5 min in liquid. Remove, shred with forks, and reserve the poaching liquid — it's now broth.

  2. 2

    Cook the vegetables. In a large pot combine kale, potato, carrot, and leek. Cover with the chicken poaching liquid (top up with water if needed). Add salt. Simmer 20–25 min until very soft.

  3. 3

    Blend the soup. Remove bay leaf / garlic if used. Blend until smooth with an immersion blender. Stir in olive oil, taste and adjust salt.

  4. 4

    Add chicken — do not boil. Return soup to low heat, add all shredded chicken, stir gently, heat 5–7 minutes only. Boiling toughens the chicken.

  5. 5

    Portion and store. Cool slightly, divide into 3 equal portions (~425g15 oz each). Refrigerate 1; freeze the other 2 in Soupercubes or airtight containers.

❄️ Freezing & reheating

Freezes up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or reheat gently on the stovetop. Medium microwave power is fine. Do not boil.

🧀 Parmesan tip

Add ~10g Parmesan per bowl only after reheating — never before freezing. It melts into the hot soup and finishes the dish.

📊 Macro note

~400–430 kcal · ~40–45g protein · ~30–35g carbs · ~9–11g fat per portion. This is a proper lean-gain dinner, not a "light soup." Parmesan not included — add ~40 kcal / 4g protein per 10g.
